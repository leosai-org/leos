# LEOS Governance

## Mission

LEOS develops an open, modular, auditable platform for coordinating AI
employees, services, tools, schedules, approvals, and persistent workflows.

## Stewardship

Bad Tech Labs LLC is the provisional project steward. Stewardship includes
maintaining the release process, protecting project identity, appointing
maintainers, administering repositories, and ensuring that public and private
materials remain properly separated.

## Maintainers

Maintainers review contributions, preserve architectural coherence, enforce
security and licensing requirements, and make release decisions. Maintainers
may be added or removed based on sustained contribution, judgment, reliability,
and project need.

Current maintainer roles are recorded in `MAINTAINERS.md` and `.github/CODEOWNERS`.

## Decision model

Routine decisions are made through reviewed pull requests and documented
issues. Significant changes should include a written rationale and address:

- compatibility and migration;
- security and privacy;
- contracts and schemas;
- licensing and provenance;
- operational impact;
- long-term maintenance.

The steward may make a final decision when consensus cannot be reached,
security or legal constraints require action, or release integrity is at risk.

## Transparency

Public source, public issues, accepted design decisions, governance policies,
and release evidence should remain public unless disclosure would expose a
vulnerability, credential, personal information, contractual restriction, or
private commercial implementation.

## Contributions

Contributions follow `CONTRIBUTING.md`, the Developer Certificate of Origin,
and `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`.

## Amendments

Governance changes should be proposed through a pull request and clearly
describe the reason, consequences, and transition plan.
