# LEOS Community Code of Conduct

LEOS is committed to a professional, welcoming, and technically rigorous
community.

## Expected conduct

Participants should:

- communicate respectfully and assume good faith;
- critique ideas and implementations rather than people;
- give actionable feedback and acknowledge uncertainty;
- respect privacy, intellectual property, and security boundaries;
- disclose conflicts of interest that affect project decisions;
- avoid harassment, discrimination, threats, intimidation, or unwanted sexual
  attention;
- avoid publishing private reports, credentials, or personal information.

## Unacceptable conduct

Unacceptable conduct includes abusive or demeaning language, targeted
harassment, deliberate disruption, impersonation, retaliation, doxxing,
security-report disclosure before coordination, and repeated bad-faith
participation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock discussions, reject
contributions, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or
permanently remove participants when necessary to protect the project and its
community.

Reports should be made privately through the repository maintainers or the
contact method published at `https://leosai.org`. Security vulnerabilities must
follow `SECURITY.md`.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented when appropriate,
and handled with respect for reporter and subject privacy.
